Little Lemon is a family-owned Mediterranean restaurant, dedicated to offering traditional recipes with a contemporary twist. Our focus is on creating an unforgettable dining experience that combines rich cultural heritage with modern culinary techniques.

Our Heritage

The history of Little Lemon traces back to the Lamon plateau, an area with a rich history dating back to prehistoric times. Evidence of early human activity includes the discovery of numerous stone and flint artifacts. Notably, in 1988, a prehistoric hunter was discovered in Val Rosna, shedding light on the lives of early settlers in the region.

More recently, fossil remains, including those of the Ursus spelaeus, a large bear once widespread across the Alpine region, were found in the San Donato cave. These fossils, now housed in the town hall, offer a glimpse into the prehistoric fauna of the area.

In 1997, middle school students unearthed artifacts such as arrowheads, scrapers, cores, and sickle blades in the hamlet of Guana. These finds, attributed to late prehistoric farmers, highlight the long-standing agricultural practices in the region.
